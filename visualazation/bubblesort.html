    About Bubble Sort!!

    The name "Bubble Sort" comes from the way the largest unsorted element "bubbles up" to its correct position in each pass or iteration of the algorithm. Here's a more detailed explanation:

    Comparison and Swapping: Bubble Sort works by repeatedly comparing adjacent elements in the list and swapping them if they are in the wrong order. In each pass, the largest element in the unsorted part of the list gradually moves to its correct position at the end of the list.

    Visual Representation: If you visualize the process of sorting as bubbles rising to the surface of a liquid, the largest unsorted element can be seen as a "bubble" that rises to the top. It "bubbles up" because, in each pass, it moves closer to its correct position by swapping with smaller adjacent elements.

    Bubble Up, Not Down: It's important to note that the elements do not move downwards in the list; they only move upwards. Elements are compared from left to right, and if two adjacent elements are out of order, they swap positions. This process continues until the largest element "bubbles up" to the end of the list, and then the second largest element does the same, and so on.

    The name "Bubble Sort" is quite intuitive because it describes the main characteristic of the algorithm: the gradual movement of elements to their correct positions by bubbling up through the list. However, it's important to note that while Bubble Sort is easy to understand, it is not the most efficient sorting algorithm and is primarily used for educational purposes or on small datasets due to its relatively poor time complexity.
